Quick Start

Click anywhere on the steel target to simulate a bullet impact. Watch the realistic physics simulation as the target swings and rotates based on where you hit it.

Controls

Left Click: Simulate a bullet impact at the clicked location. The target will respond with realistic physics based on the impact point, including both linear motion and rotation.

Reset Target: Returns the target to its initial resting position and clears all velocities and impact history.

Physics Simulation

Target Configuration: IPSC-style steel target with rectangular body (18" Ã— 30") and head (6" Ã— 9"), made of 0.5" thick steel.

Chain Mounting: Two chains attached at 45-degree angles from the shoulders to overhead anchor points. Chains act as springs with realistic tension forces.

Impact Physics: Bullet impacts transfer momentum to the target, causing both linear motion and rotation. The exact response depends on where you hit the target relative to its center of mass.

Damping: Air resistance and internal friction gradually slow down the target motion, causing it to eventually return to rest.

Constraints: The chain springs pull the target back toward its resting position, creating realistic swinging behavior.

What to Observe

Center Hits: Hitting near the center of mass causes mostly linear motion with minimal rotation.

Edge Hits: Hitting near the edges causes significant rotation as well as linear motion.

Head vs Body: The head is lighter and offset, so hits there create different dynamics than body hits.

Chain Dynamics: Watch how the chains stretch and pull the target back, creating oscillating motion.

Statistics Display

Linear Velocity: Overall speed of the target's center of mass (m/s).

Angular Velocity: Rotation speed around the target's center of mass (rad/s).

Position X/Z: Current position of the target's center of mass. X is downrange, Z is vertical.

Target Mass: Total mass of the steel target in kilograms.
