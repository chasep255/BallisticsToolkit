Overview

F-Class Simulator is an interactive 3D shooting simulator that recreates the experience of long-range F-Class competition shooting. Practice reading wind (mirage and flags), managing ballistics with spin effects, and making precise shots at standard F-Class distances from 300 to 1000 yards with correct targets and authentic match scoring.

Ballistic Parameters

BC (Ballistic Coefficient): Aerodynamic efficiency. Higher = less wind drift and drop

Drag Function: G7 for modern boat-tail bullets, G1 for flat-base or older projectiles

MV (Muzzle Velocity): Initial velocity in fps. Critical for trajectory and time-of-flight

Diameter: Bullet caliber in inches. Affects line-breaking scoring and spin calculations

Weight (gr): Bullet weight in grains. Required for accurate spin moment of inertia calculations

Length (in): Bullet length in inches. Used for moment of inertia and spin aerodynamics

Twist (in/turn): Barrel twist rate (e.g., 8 for 1:8 twist). Used to calculate bullet spin rate from muzzle velocity

MV œÉ (Standard Deviation): Velocity variation in fps. Lower = more consistent. Default: 7 fps

Accuracy (MOA): Rifle+shooter precision. 1 MOA ‚âà 1 inch at 100 yards. Default: 0.25 MOA

Spin Effects: Spin drift is zeroed during setup. Crosswind jump is simulated when wind direction or speed changes, causing a brief jump in trajectory before the bullet stabilizes.

Technical Notes

Trajectory: RK2 integration with a 4DOF state. Spin effects are simplified and empirically tuned; spin drift is removed during zeroing, crosswind jump is modeled as a short transient when wind changes.

Zeroing: Iterative algorithm correcting for y/z error at the target plane. Runs in zero wind for repeatability and converges quickly.

Coordinates & Units: Three.js world in yards (X=right, Y=up, Z=toward camera/‚àíZ downrange). Wrappers convert to the C++ simulation frame (X=downrange, Y=right, Z=up) and meters internally.

Wind Model: 2D curl-noise field with multiple octaves and global advection. RMS-normalized at init for stable magnitudes across presets. Flags and mirage sample this same field over space and time.

Mirage: World-anchored distortion sampling predominantly in the last quarter of the range to emphasize target-area cues; apparent flow aligns with crosswind direction.

Tips for Success

- Start with calm wind conditions to learn the basics
- Use the spotting scope to observe flags, mirage, and shot impacts
- Compare mirage and flags at different distances for wind gradients
- Watch the red shot marker to see your last impact
- Make small adjustments to your aim point (0.1" increments)
- Pay attention to wind flag behavior along the entire range
- Higher BC bullets drift less in the wind
- Lower MV standard deviation = more consistent impacts
- Practice at shorter distances before attempting 1000 yards
