Overview

F-Class Simulator is an interactive 3D shooting simulator that recreates the experience of long-range F-Class competition shooting. Practice reading wind, managing ballistics, and making precise shots at standard F-Class distances from 300 to 1000 yards.

Controls

Spotting Scope (Bottom-Left):
- WASD keys: Pan and tilt the scope view
- E key: Zoom in (increase magnification)
- Q key: Zoom out (decrease magnification)
- Range: 2x to 100x magnification
- Movement speed scales with zoom level (slower when zoomed in)
- Use to observe impacts and read wind mirage

Rifle Scope (Bottom-Right):
- ↑↓←→ Arrow keys: Adjust aim point (0.1 MOA per press)
- += keys: Zoom in (5% per press)
- -_ keys: Zoom out (5% per press)
- Red crosshair shows current aim point
- Shows mirage for wind reading at target

Firing:
- Space bar: Fire a shot
- Each shot is recorded and scored automatically
- Red marker shows last shot impact
- HUD shows current score and statistics
- Match completes after 60 shots (standard F-Class match)

Match-Style Target Animation:
- After firing, target lowers into the pits (~2.7 seconds)
- Target lingers in pits for 3 seconds (scoring/marking)
- Target raises with new spotter marker (~2.7 seconds)
- Cannot fire while target is down (~8.3 second cycle)
- Your aim point stays fixed during target animation
- Simulates realistic F-Class match conditions

Wind Reading

Wind Flags:
- Observe flag angle and flapping to estimate wind speed
- Flags point in the direction wind is blowing
- Watch flags at multiple distances for wind shear

Mirage (Heat Shimmer):
- Essential for reading wind at long range
- Visible through both scopes (higher zoom = more mirage)
- Constantly boils upward (vertical convection)
- Crosswind causes horizontal drift of the pattern

Mirage Behavior by Wind Speed:
- < 3 mph: Vertical boil dominates (straight up)
- 3-6 mph: Mixed vertical/horizontal shimmer (leaning)
- > 8 mph: Horizontal flow dominates, boil fades

Reading Direction:
- Left drift = right-to-left wind (hold right)
- Right drift = left-to-right wind (hold left)
- Mirage shows average wind biased toward target

Wind Presets:
- DeadCalm: < 0.5 mph (essentially no wind)
- Calm: 1-3 mph (light, smooth conditions)
- LightBreeze: 2-4 mph (gentle, steady)
- Moderate: 3-8 mph (realistic match conditions) - DEFAULT
- Strong: 8-15 mph (difficult, steady wind)
- Variable: 2-8 mph (highly variable, multiple scales)
- Shear: 2-5 mph (direction changes over range)
- Switchy: 1-3 mph (frequent direction changes)
- Gusty: 2-8 mph (sudden strong gusts with calm periods)
- Steady: 3-6 mph (consistent with minimal variation)
- Turbulent: 2-6 mph (chaotic with rapid changes)
- Mirage: 0.8-2 mph (very light, good for heat mirage)

Strategy:
- Use spotting scope to scan entire range
- Compare flags and mirage at different distances
- Watch mirage over rocks, bushes, and markers
- Look for wind gradients (changes along range)
- Make corrections based on previous impacts

F-Class Competition

Standard Distances: 300, 500, 600, 800, 900, and 1000 yards

Target Types: NRA F-Class targets (MR63-FCA, MR65-FCA, MR1-FCA, LR-FCA) automatically selected by distance

Scoring: Standard NRA scoring with 10-ring (X-ring) precision. Bullet diameter affects line-breaking

Match Format: 60-shot match (standard F-Class competition)

Dropped Points: Points missed (10 - score per shot) and X-rings missed (1 - X per shot)

Ballistic Parameters

BC (Ballistic Coefficient): Aerodynamic efficiency (0.001-1.0). Higher = less wind drift and drop

Drag Function: G7 for modern boat-tail bullets, G1 for flat-base or older projectiles

MV (Muzzle Velocity): Initial velocity in fps. Critical for trajectory and time-of-flight

Diameter: Bullet caliber in inches. Affects line-breaking scoring

MV σ (Standard Deviation): Velocity variation in fps. Lower = more consistent. Default: 7 fps

Accuracy (MOA): Rifle+shooter precision. 1 MOA ≈ 1 inch at 100 yards. Default: 0.25 MOA

HUD (Top-Right Overlay)

Target: Current target number

Shots: Current shot count / 60 total

Score: Total score in "XXX-Yx" format (points-X count)

Dropped: Missed points and X-rings

Last Shot: Most recent shot score with X indicator

MV: Actual muzzle velocity of last shot (with variation)

Impact V: Bullet velocity at target

FPS: Rendering frame rate

Tips for Success

- Start with calm wind conditions to learn the basics
- Use the spotting scope to observe flags, mirage, and shot impacts
- Compare mirage and flags at different distances for wind gradients
- Watch the red shot marker to see your last impact
- Make small adjustments to your aim point (0.1" increments)
- Pay attention to wind flag behavior along the entire range
- Higher BC bullets drift less in the wind
- Lower MV standard deviation = more consistent impacts
- Practice at shorter distances before attempting 1000 yards
