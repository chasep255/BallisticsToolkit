This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect when you use Ballistics Toolkit, how we use and share it, and your choices regarding that information.

Ballistics Toolkit is a free, non‑commercial, static web application. The site owner does not maintain user accounts, mailing lists, or a server‑side database of personal information.

By accessing or using the Services, you consent to the collection, use, and disclosure of your information as described here. If you do not agree, please do not use the Services.

Ballistics Toolkit collects only limited data:

Usage data via Google Analytics: When you visit the site, we use Google Analytics to collect pseudonymous information such as pages visited, referrer URLs, approximate geographic location based on IP address, device and browser type, and interaction events (e.g., clicks). IP addresses may be anonymized according to your region and Google's settings.

Cookies and local storage: The site uses essential cookies for core functionality (such as remembering your preferences) and analytics cookies for Google Analytics. We do not use advertising or marketing cookies.

Feedback: If you voluntarily contact us (for example, by opening an issue on GitHub), we may collect your username and any information you provide. We do not operate contact forms or email subscriptions.

We do not intentionally collect names, mailing addresses, payment details, or other personally identifying information.

3. How We Use Information

We use analytics data to understand how visitors interact with our website, to diagnose technical issues, and to improve functionality.

We may aggregate and anonymize usage data for statistical or research purposes.

We do not sell, rent, or trade user information.

Google Analytics is our analytics provider. Google processes information on our behalf to provide aggregated usage statistics. You can learn more in Google's Privacy Policy and its partner‑site policy.

We may share aggregated, non‑identifiable usage statistics in open‑source project reports.

We do not otherwise share personal data with third parties.

Privacy laws such as the GDPR or CCPA grant individuals the right to access, correct, or delete personal data. Because we hold no personal data on our servers, we cannot directly fulfill such requests; however, we encourage users to use the methods above to manage their data.
